## WHAT THIS MEANS

**Strategic Interpretation:**
Struinova's website is **credibility-first but search-second**. The homepage and about page tell a compelling story (resident model, cold cases, 30-year track record, Basadur methodology) — but that story is buried in interactive design and narrative prose, not indexed for the keywords decision-makers are searching.

Competitors (Stonehill, Six Paths, Voltage Control) have won the SEO game by:
1. Creating service-focused landing pages for each offering
2. Building blog/resource ecosystems (10-25 pages each) for thought leadership + keyword coverage
3. Establishing training/certification programs (lead magnets + recurring revenue)
4. Doing geographic targeting (Stonehill owns 15 city markets)

Struinova can leapfrog by:
1. **Leveraging unique credibility** (cold cases, 30-year ops history, resident model) — competitors can't claim this
2. **Focusing on Simplexity methodology** — Basadur-certified, others teach generic design thinking
3. **Going local first** (Indianapolis is undefended; Darin Eich is the only real threat)
4. **Building blog + resources** (quick 2-3 posts/month captures long-tail traffic)

**30-Day Priorities (Quick Wins):**
1. "First Principles Thinking Training" service page (1,500-2,000 words)
2. "Indianapolis Innovation Consulting" local service page (1,000 words)
3. "Creative Problem-Solving Workshops" service page (1,500-2,000 words)
4. Migrate 3 embedded case studies → blog posts (3 × 800-1,000 words)

**60-Day Priorities (Expand Thin Content):**
1. Expand lens pages (creative problem-solving, first principles, design thinking, applied AI) → 1,500-2,000 word service pages each
2. Add 4-6 blog posts on facilitation, innovation, problem-solving methodologies
3. "Cold Case Problem-Solving" thought leadership article (2,000-2,500 words)

**90-Day Priorities (Systematize):**
1. Establish 2-3 blog posts/month schedule (field_notes section)
2. Consider training offering page ("Innovation Facilitation Certification" or similar)
3. Add geographic pages (at minimum: "Chicago," "New York," "Toronto," "London")
